\section{Project Overview}
CelestiaVR is a Meta Quest 3 VR stargazing application that places the user on a
tropical island at night and allows them to explore the sky --- stars, planets,
constellations, deep-sky objects, and the Milky Way --- using gaze and controller
interactions inspired by Star Walk 2.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Week 12 Progress}

\subsection{Topic Reconsideration}
Midway through this sprint the team briefly reconsidered pivoting to a
\textit{Rage Room} VR concept due to concerns about the technical scope of
CelestiaVR. After consulting with the professor, we concluded that while
CelestiaVR is ambitious, it is the stronger and more original project.
We committed to continuing with CelestiaVR for the remainder of the course.

\subsection{What Was Implemented This Week}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Island Environment} --- Replaced the placeholder floor with a
    fully textured tropical island (GLTF/GLB model) including mesh collider for
    VR locomotion.

    \item \textbf{Milky Way Skybox} --- Integrated an ESO equirectangular
    panoramic image (\textit{eso0932a}) as a Unity \texttt{Skybox/Panoramic}
    material. The skybox rotation is driven by the SkyManager's sidereal time
    calculation so the Milky Way band moves accurately with time of day.

    \item \textbf{Planet Gaze \& Dwell Inspection} --- Implemented a full dwell
    selection pipeline: the user gazes at a planet for 3 seconds, triggering a
    hologram pull-out animation that brings the planet to a comfortable viewing
    distance. The hologram rotates in place while an information panel fades in.

    \item \textbf{Hologram Dismissal} --- Added a dismiss interaction (controller
    Activate button) to send the hologram away and return to the sky view.

    \item \textbf{Time Scroll Controller} --- Built a Star Walk 2-style time
    scrubbing system driven by the right thumbstick. Horizontal input advances or
    rewinds simulated time; releasing the thumbstick applies inertia/momentum.
    Holding the grip button applies a 24$\times$ speed multiplier for jumping
    days or weeks. All sky elements (stars, constellations, planets, Milky Way)
    update in real time with the scrubbed time.

    \item \textbf{Deep Sky Objects} --- Andromeda (M31), Orion Nebula (M42),
    Pleiades (M45), and Hercules Cluster (M13) are rendered as billboard sprites
    positioned at their correct RA/Dec coordinates, rotating with the sky.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What Was Temporarily Removed}
The \textbf{telescope interaction} and several secondary controller interactions
were disabled this week. These features were introduced in the Week 11 build but
caused stability issues and conflicted with the new dwell/inspection pipeline.
Rather than ship broken interactions, we chose to cleanly remove them and
re-implement them properly in Week 13.

\section{Usability \& Evaluation Plan}

\subsection{Goals}
The primary usability goal is to verify that a first-time VR user can:
(1) understand how to select a celestial object without any tutorial,
(2) read and absorb information from the inspection panel, and
(3) successfully dismiss the hologram and continue exploring.

\subsection{Participants}
We plan to recruit 4--6 participants from the class with no prior experience
with CelestiaVR. At least two participants should have minimal VR experience
to capture first-time-user behaviour.

\subsection{Tasks}
Each participant will be asked to complete the following tasks without
assistance:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item \textit{Find and inspect a planet} --- identify any planet in the sky
    and trigger the dwell inspection.
    \item \textit{Read the panel} --- state aloud one fact shown on the
    information panel.
    \item \textit{Dismiss the hologram} --- return the hologram to the sky.
    \item \textit{Scroll time forward} --- use the thumbstick to advance the sky
    by several hours and observe the change.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Metrics}
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item \textbf{Task completion rate} --- \% of participants who complete each
    task unassisted.
    \item \textbf{Time on task} --- seconds taken to trigger first successful
    dwell selection.
    \item \textbf{Error count} --- number of accidental dismissals or failed
    dwell attempts.
    \item \textbf{Comfort rating} --- 1--5 Likert scale for motion comfort and
    visual clarity.
    \item \textbf{Qualitative feedback} --- short post-session interview (3--5
    minutes) asking what felt intuitive and what was confusing.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Success Criteria}
We consider the interaction loop successful if:
\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item $\geq$80\% of participants complete Tasks 1--3 without assistance.
    \item Average time to first dwell selection is under 60 seconds.
    \item No participant reports motion sickness above a 2/5 rating.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Timeline}
Playtesting is scheduled for Week 13. Findings will inform refinements to dwell
timer duration, panel layout, and the re-introduction of the telescope
interaction.
